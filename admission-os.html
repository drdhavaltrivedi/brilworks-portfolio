01 — Overview

What we built

Admission OS is a multi-agent AI platform that coaches MBA and Masters applicants through the full application lifecycle. Instead of a single chatbot, the platform orchestrates seven specialized AI agents around a central intelligence layer called the Narrative Anchor Object (NAO) — a durable, machine-readable representation of the applicant's story, values, and evidence base.

Eight document modules cover the full admissions surface: essays, resume, recommendation letters, transcripts, scholarship applications, short-answer responses, interview prep, and cross-school comparison. Every module reads from and writes to the NAO, so claims, numbers, and stories stay consistent across all artifacts.

The platform was designed for Open Admits, a boutique admissions consulting firm with 130+ admits to top schools (HBS, Wharton, Stanford, Kellogg, Columbia, MIT Sloan, INSEAD, LBS) and over $7M in scholarships secured. Admission OS replicates the firm's narrative-first methodology as a scalable, self-serve software product.

B2C

Direct to applicants

B2B

White-label for firms

Consulting firms use Admission OS as their AI backend — students self-serve the first 80%, consultants handle the final 20% with full AI context.

02 — The problem

Why the market needed this

The MBA admissions consulting industry is a $500M+ market with a fundamental scaling problem. The best consultants charge $5,000–$15,000 per student and handle 20–40 clients per year. The demand is global — particularly from India, China, and the Middle East — but the supply of expert consultants is fixed.

For applicants

Top consultants cost $5K–$15K for 3 schools. Self-serve platforms ($349) offer static content with no strategic intelligence. AI tools ($20–40/mo) score essays but don't build positioning, challenge logic, or analyse cross-document consistency. Generic AI (ChatGPT) produces output that any admissions officer can spot.

For consulting firms

Consultants spend 40–50% of their time on first-draft review and basic feedback — work AI can do. Each consultant manages 20–40 students/year, creating a hard revenue ceiling. Scaling means hiring more consultants, which is expensive and slow.

For Open Admits specifically

Sridhar Yendamuri (founder) personally coaches each student through positioning, essay strategy, document review, and interview prep. His methodology has produced 130+ admits to top schools. But his time is the bottleneck. He needed to encode his consulting process into software — without losing the strategic depth that differentiates Open Admits from competitors.

The core insight: Existing AI tools grade your essay. They don't build your positioning, challenge your goal logic, restructure your narrative arc, match your voice, or analyse your entire application for contradictions. The gap between "essay grader" and "admissions consultant" is the gap Admission OS fills.

03 — The solution

A consulting team that runs in 15 seconds

Admission OS replaces the linear "submit → score → rewrite" pattern used by every AI tool on the market with a multi-agent consulting workflow:

Old

Before: prompt-based scoring

Student submits essay → AI scores on rubric → AI suggests rewrites → student edits → repeat. No strategy, no memory, no cross-document awareness.

New

After: agent-orchestrated consulting

Student enters → Strategy Agent builds positioning → Framework Agent validates logic → Story Architect restructures → Strategic Advisor prioritises → Editing Agent elevates → Gap Agent advises across the full application.

The system operates in two layers:

Layer 1 — Scoring (objective evaluation): Rubric-based dimension scores (leadership, impact, specificity, school fit). Deterministic and repeatable. Same input always produces the same scores.

Layer 2 — Agents (strategic intelligence): Each agent has a distinct role, structured JSON input/output, memory of prior interactions, and the ability to issue decision gate warnings. Agents don't just evaluate — they challenge, restructure, and advise.

04 — The 7-agent pipeline

Each agent has a distinct role

Every agent receives the Narrative Anchor Object as context, produces structured JSON output, and passes that output to the next agent in the chain. Agents run on Google Gemini 2.5 — Flash for real-time foreground calls, Pro for deeper on-demand analysis.

S

Strategy Agent

Builds positioning before any writing begins
Reads the student's 30-question consultation form and builds the Narrative Anchor Object — the foundational intelligence document that every downstream agent consumes. Identifies the student's unique differentiators, maps stories to essay archetypes, validates the goal bridge logic, and produces per-school fit arguments with specific professors, clubs, and courses to reference.
Receives30 consultation answers, target schools, story inventory, test scores
ProducesNAO: positioning statement, narrative thread, differentiators, school fit mapping, goal bridge, story-to-essay allocation
F

Framework Agent

Validates logic — the tough consultant Soft gate
Challenges every claim in the student's essay against their NAO positioning. Tests goal bridge logic ("Why consulting? Why not just get promoted?"), school fit specificity ("Could this paragraph appear in 5 other applications?"), and reflection depth ("Is this insight specific to your story, or could any applicant write it?"). Communicates admissions frameworks to teach the student how to think like an admissions committee.
ReceivesNAO + essay text + scoring results + school config
ProducesChallenges (severity-rated) + logic score + frameworks referenced + pass/warn verdict
A

Story Architect

Restructures narrative — does not rewrite Soft gate
Analyses the essay's architecture: the 4-layer balance (Context / Conflict / Action / Impact), the narrative arc (chronological vs impact-first vs tension-first), and story fitness (is this the right story from the bank for this essay type?). Forces restructuring before any line-level editing happens. If the strongest moment is buried in paragraph 3, the Story Architect says "move it to the opening."
ReceivesNAO + segmented essay + story bank + Framework output
ProducesLayer balance analysis, arc recommendation, restructuring directives, story fitness check
$

Scoring Engine

4-dimension parallel scoring — runs alongside Story Architect
Fires four scoring dimensions simultaneously via parallel execution: leadership signals, impact quantification, specificity and voice authenticity, and school fit. All four dimensions share a single cached instruction prefix (14,000 tokens) to minimise cost — each dimension appends only a short differentiator. Produces the numeric scores that appear on the student's results screen.
ReceivesNAO + segmented essay + school rubric weights
ProducesPer-dimension scores (0–10) + inline feedback + total /100
★

Strategic Advisor

The merge point — synthesises scoring + structure
Receives both the Scoring Engine's dimension scores AND the Story Architect's structural analysis. Synthesises into: the top 3 priorities for this draft, 2 competitive risks ("this essay sounds like 500 other applicants from India in fintech"), and a rewrite blueprint that tells the Editing Agent exactly which sections to focus on and what approach to take.
ReceivesScoring Engine output + Story Architect output + NAO
ProducesTop 3 priorities, 2 competitive risks, rewrite blueprint
E

Editing Agent

The only agent that touches the actual words
Follows the Strategic Advisor's rewrite blueprint to generate line-level improvements for the 2–3 weakest sections. Every rewrite matches the student's voice fingerprint — if the student writes in short, declarative sentences with concrete imagery, the rewrites sound like them, not like a consultant. Always provides 2 options: Option A (identity/reflection focus) and Option B (impact/evidence focus). Never invents facts.
ReceivesNAO + voice fingerprint + Advisor's blueprint + essay text
ProducesPer-section rewrites (original + Option A + Option B + reasoning)
G

Gap Analysis Agent

The senior advisor — sees the entire application
Activated after 2+ documents are complete. Reads the entire application — every essay, the resume, rec letter briefing, transcripts, all agent decisions, all scores — and identifies gaps, contradictions, and missed opportunities. "Your essay says 'led a team of 8' but your consultation form says 'cross-functional coordination' — which is it?" Produces prioritised next actions and an application health score.
ReceivesNAO + all documents + all evaluations + story bank + school targets
ProducesHealth score, gaps, contradictions, cross-school insights, prioritised next actions
05 — Execution flow

What happens when a student clicks "Evaluate"

The orchestration engine is a directed acyclic graph (DAG) with parallel branches and merge points. Steps 3a and 3b run simultaneously. Steps 6a and 6b run simultaneously. The Strategic Advisor (step 5) is the merge point where both analysis branches converge.

0ms

Load context

Fetch student profile, current NAO, and all agent memories from Supabase. No AI call.

~1.5s

Segmenter

Gemini Flash splits the essay into Context / Conflict / Action / Impact sections with word counts and percentages.

~3–4s

Scoring Engine (parallel branch A)

4 scoring dimensions fire via Promise.all() with a shared 14K-token cached instruction prefix. Produces per-dimension scores.

~2–3s

Story Architect (parallel branch B)

Analyses narrative arc, 4-layer balance, story fitness. Runs simultaneously with Scoring Engine.

~2–3s

Framework Gate

Validates goal bridge, school fit, claim support. Soft warns on weak logic — never blocks in MVP. Dismissable per-claim.

~2–3s

Strategic Advisor (merge point)

Receives BOTH scoring results AND structural analysis. Synthesises into priorities + risks + rewrite blueprint.

~3–4s

Editing Agent (parallel branch A)

Follows the Advisor's blueprint. Generates voice-matched rewrites for the 2–3 weakest sections. Two options per section.

~3–4s

Gap Agent (parallel branch B, conditional)

Only fires if 2+ documents complete. Cross-document analysis: gaps, contradictions, next actions, health score.

0ms

Persist + return

All agent outputs saved to Supabase. Evaluation record created (immutable). Results pushed to client via real-time subscription.

06 — The Narrative Anchor Object

The shared brain behind every agent

The NAO is a structured JSON document created by the Strategy Agent and consumed by every downstream agent. It represents the student's core positioning, narrative thread, differentiators, school fit mapping, goal bridge logic, and story allocation. It's versioned and immutable — every change creates a new version with a diff trail.

Without the NAO, each agent would operate in isolation. With it, the Framework Agent knows which positioning to validate against, the Story Architect knows which stories are allocated where, the Editing Agent knows which voice to match, and the Gap Agent knows what the entire application should look like.

NAO fields

positioning_statement — who this student uniquely is
core_narrative_thread — the through-line across all docs
key_differentiators[] — 3–5 unique, evidence-backed traits
school_fit_mapping{} — per-school fit + culture signals
goal_bridge_logic — current → short-term → MBA → long-term
gaps_to_address[] — weaknesses + mitigation strategies
recommended_archetypes{} — story → essay type mapping
voice_fingerprint — sentence length, vocab, tone patterns
07 — Technology stack

Built for speed, cost, and scale

Google Gemini API

2.5 Flash for foreground agents (sub-1.5s). 2.5 Pro for on-demand deep analysis. Context caching for 90% cost reduction on repeat calls.

Next.js 14

App Router with Server Components. API Routes for all AI calls. Middleware for auth + tier gating. Edge runtime on Vercel.

Supabase

PostgreSQL with Row Level Security. Edge functions for agent pipeline. Real-time subscriptions for evaluation progress. Auth via email + Google OAuth.

TipTap Editor

Custom extensions for agent annotation marks, accept/reject rewrite UI, section-based editing, and inline highlighting.

Upstash Redis

School config cache, rubric data, agent instruction blocks. 5-min TTL for on-demand, 1-hour for foreground. Serverless-compatible.

Vercel

Global CDN. Edge runtime for spend cap enforcement. Environment-per-branch. Automatic preview deploys on PR.

Stripe

One-time tier purchases. Webhook-driven access control. 5 lifecycle events handled with idempotency keys.

Whisper API

Voice-to-draft and interview transcription. Browser mic → Whisper → text. Full tier only.

Cal.com

Consultant session booking. NAO auto-populated in consultant view. Zero cold-start conversations.

08 — AI economics

96% gross margin at $397

The original project brief estimated $1.16 per student. Production-quality agent prompts require 8,000–20,000 token instruction blocks — not the minimal 1,500-token assumptions in early estimates. The realistic blended cost is approximately $17 per student with proper prompt caching, rising to ~$45 without caching.

~$17
Blended cost per student (with caching)
~$45
Without caching (3x higher)
95.8%
AI gross margin at $397 tier

Non-negotiable: Prompt caching must be verified live on every agent endpoint before Phase 3 signoff. Cache hit rate must exceed 80% on repeat calls within TTL. Without it, per-student cost triples and the business model is at risk.

Cost controls built into the platform:

Per-user daily spend cap of $1.50/day enforced at the edge function level. Auto-downgrade to Gemini Flash when 80% of cap is reached. Overnight Gap Analysis uses Gemini Batch API for 50% cost reduction. Document-change-gated cron ensures Gap Analysis only re-runs when documents actually change.

09 — UI/UX design approach

Calm, structured, professional

The student is anxious about their application. The UI should feel like a calm, professional consulting office — not a flashy tech product. Muted colours, generous white space, clear typography. Agent feedback feels distinct but not gamified. No cartoon avatars. No chat bubbles. Professional annotations.

Progressive disclosure

Only the consultation form is active on first login. Other modules unlock after NAO creation. The student is never overwhelmed by 8 modules at once.

Speed feedback

15–20 second pipeline shows progress with agent avatars lighting up. Foreground agents annotate inline within 1.2 seconds. Never a blank spinner.

Unified results screen

Students see one cohesive evaluation — scores on the left, challenges in the centre, rewrites on the right, next actions at the bottom. Never raw agent JSON.

Tier awareness

Locked features show as greyed-out with upgrade CTA, not hidden. Students see what they're missing to drive upgrades from Free → Core → Full.
